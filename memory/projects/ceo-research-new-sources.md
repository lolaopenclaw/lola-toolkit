## 🔄 4. Counter-Arguments: PMs Are MORE Needed

### Andrew Ng: Product Management as the New Bottleneck
**Sources:**  
- HackerNoon: https://hackernoon.com/andrew-ng-product-team-ratios-evolving-to-just-one-software-developer-for-every-two-product-manager
- Bagel AI: https://bagel.ai/blog/andrew-ng-is-right-product-management-is-the-bottleneck-heres-what-comes-next/

**Ng's Argument:**
- Traditional ratio: 1 PM to 6 engineers (or 4-8 engineers)
- **NEW ratio prediction:** 1 PM to 0.5 engineers (2 PMs per engineer!)
- Reasoning: "Writing software is becoming cheaper. This will lead to increased demand for people who can decide what to build."

**Key Quote:**  
> "Yesterday, a team proposed a ratio of one product manager to 0.5 engineers… That would have sounded absurd a year ago." — Andrew Ng, Y Combinator Startup School

**Counter-Quote (Engineer Perspective):**  
> "Two PMs for one engineer? This is my actual nightmare." — Reddit developer

**Analysis:**
While Ng's 2:1 ratio is extreme, his core insight is valid: **As coding becomes commoditized via AI, product strategy becomes the bottleneck.** The "what to build" question becomes more valuable than the "how to build" execution.

**Relevance:** High-credibility voice (Andrew Ng, Stanford AI professor, Coursera co-founder) arguing PMs are MORE critical in AI era, not less. Strong counter to "AI will replace PMs" narrative.
