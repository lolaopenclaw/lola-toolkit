## Contradictions & Tensions — Reconciled

### Andrew Ng (2:1 ratio) vs Claire Vo (task automation)

**Apparent contradiction:**
- Ng: "Need MORE PMs" (2 PMs per 1 engineer)
- Vo: "PMs automate tasks, fewer needed"

**Reconciliation:**
- Ng talks about **product strategy velocity** (decisions are bottleneck)
- Vo talks about **individual PM capacity** (each PM does more)
- **Both true:** FEWER traditional PMs, but remaining ones are MUCH more powerful
- **Net effect:** Flat or slightly reduced headcount, massively increased output per PM

**Implication for IONOS:** Don't hire 2x PMs. Upskill existing PMs to 2-3x effectiveness.

### Gartner 95% Fail vs BCG 36K GPTs Success

**Apparent contradiction:**
- Gartner/MIT: 95% of AI initiatives fail to generate ROI
- BCG: 36,000 GPTs deployed successfully

**Reconciliation:**
- BCG is the **5% that succeeded**
- What differentiates BCG: **Product organization structure** (orchestrators, governance, UX center of excellence)
- This PROVES the thesis: **Without AI PMs/orchestrators, you're in the 95%**

**Implication for IONOS:** The 95% failure rate is the REASON to invest in AI PM evolution, not a reason to avoid AI.

### Traditional Scrum (Garzás) vs Modern Agile

**No real contradiction:**
- Garzás: "Scrum is obsolete for AI-native teams"
- Industry: Still using agile principles

**Reconciliation:**
- Discovery + Delivery **persist** (Garzás confirms this)
- **HOW** you execute those phases changes (AI-native methods)
- Sprints → Continuous loops
- Standups → Dashboards
- PRs → Experiment logs

**Implication:** Don't throw away agile. Evolve it into AI-native product management (AIPM).
