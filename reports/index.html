❓ What is WAL?

Simple Explanation

WAL (Write-Ahead Logging) is like a safety net for your system.

Imagine you're editing a document and the power goes out. Without WAL, you lose everything. With WAL, you have a record of every action you took, so when power returns, you can recover.

How It Works

Step 1: REGISTER
"I'm going to change X from A to B" (write to log with SHA256 checksum)

Step 2: APPLY
Execute the change (X = B)

Step 3: RECOVER
If crash happens, replay from log on reboot ✅

Components in OpenClaw

  • 📝 Daily Logs: Each change recorded with timestamp + SHA256
  • 📸 Snapshots (6h): Complete system state snapshot every 6 hours
  • ❄️ COLD Archive: Old snapshots compressed (83% space savings)
  • 🔄 Recovery System: Automatic validation + replay on crash

Why It Matters for You

✅ If VPS crashes → recover from last snapshot (max 6h old)
✅ All data protected → SHA256 verified
✅ Automatic recovery → zero manual work
